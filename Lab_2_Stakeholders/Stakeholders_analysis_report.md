# STAKEHOLDER ANALYSIS REPORT
## Bus Ticketing System

---

### 1. Introduction

#### 1.1 Project Overview
The city transport authority aims to develop a **Bus Ticketing System** to replace the current manual ticketing process. Passengers currently face long queues at bus terminals and uncertainty about seat availability. Bus operators struggle with manual seat allocation, revenue tracking, and schedule communication.

#### 1.2 Purpose of This Report
This report identifies and analyzes all stakeholders involved in the Bus Ticketing System project. It categorizes stakeholders based on their power and interest, defines their responsibilities, and recommends communication strategies to ensure project success.

#### 1.3 Project Scenario
The current bus ticketing process in the city is entirely manual. Passengers must physically visit bus terminals to purchase tickets, often facing long queues and uncertainty about seat availability. Bus operators struggle to manage seat allocations, track revenue, and communicate schedule changes to passengers.

The city transport authority wants to develop a **Bus Ticketing System** that will:
- Allow passengers to search for bus routes and schedules online
- Allow passengers to book and purchase tickets online
- Allow passengers to select and reserve specific seats
- Allow bus operators to manage bus schedules, routes, and pricing
- Allow bus operators to track revenue and generate reports
- Send automatic booking confirmations and reminders to passengers
- Authenticate users (passengers and operators) before allowing access

### 2. Stakeholder Identification

| **Stakeholder** | **Role/Description** |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Passenger** | Searches for bus routes, books tickets, selects seats, makes payments, receives confirmations and reminders. |
| **Bus Operator** | Manages bus schedules, routes, pricing, and seat allocations; tracks revenue. |
| **Bus Company Owner** | Owns the bus company; oversees operations, revenue, and profitability. |
| **Transport Authority** | Government body that regulates bus operations, ensures compliance with safety and pricing regulations. |
| **IT Department** | Installs and maintains the system, manages servers and databases, ensures system security and uptime. |
| **System Developer** | Builds, tests, and deploys the system. |
| **System Tester/QA** | Tests the system for bugs and ensures quality. |
| **Payment Gateway Provider** | Processes online payments (e.g., bank, mobile money, credit card). |
| **Bus Driver** | Operates the bus; receives passenger lists and seating information from the system. |
| **Customer Support Staff** | Handles passenger complaints, refunds, and assistance with bookings. |
| **External Vendors** | Provide hardware, software licenses, or cloud hosting services. |

---

### 3. Stakeholder Responsibilities

| **Stakeholder** | **Responsibilities** |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Passenger** | Search for routes; book tickets; select seats; make payments; receive confirmations; cancel or reschedule bookings (if allowed). |
| **Bus Operator** | Add, update, and delete bus schedules; manage routes and pricing; monitor seat availability; view revenue reports. |
| **Bus Company Owner** | Approve pricing strategies; review revenue and performance reports; make business decisions. |
| **Transport Authority** | Approve bus routes and pricing; ensure compliance with regulations; monitor safety standards. |
| **IT Department** | Ensure system infrastructure (servers, network) is available; manage system backups and security. |
| **System Developer** | Develop and deploy the system; fix bugs; implement new features. |
| **System Tester/QA** | Test the system for bugs; ensure system reliability and usability. |
| **Payment Gateway Provider** | Process online payments securely; ensure transaction reliability. |
| **Bus Driver** | Access passenger lists; confirm passenger boarding; report issues. |
| **Customer Support Staff** | Assist passengers with booking issues; process refunds; handle complaints. |
| **External Vendors** | Supply hardware, software, or cloud hosting; provide technical support. |

---

### 4. Power-Interest Matrix


| **Quadrant** | **Stakeholders** | **Strategy** |
|--------------|------------------|--------------|
| **High Power, High Interest** | Bus Operator, Bus Company Owner, Transport Authority | Manage closely; involve in meetings, decision-making, and approvals |
| **High Power, Low Interest** | IT Department, Payment Gateway Provider | Keep satisfied; consult on technical and payment integration matters |
| **Low Power, High Interest** | Passengers, Bus Driver, Customer Support Staff | Keep informed; conduct surveys, feedback sessions, and training |
| **Low Power, Low Interest** | External Vendors, System Testers | Monitor; communicate only when necessary |

---


### 5. Conclusion

This stakeholder analysis report has successfully identified all key stakeholders involved in the **Bus Ticketing System** project. The stakeholders have been categorized based on their power and interest levels using the Power-Interest Matrix, and appropriate communication strategies have been recommended for each group.

**Key Findings:**
- **High Power, High Interest** stakeholders (Bus Operator, Bus Company Owner, Transport Authority) require close management and active involvement in all major decisions.
- **High Power, Low Interest** stakeholders (IT Department, Payment Gateway Provider) need to be kept satisfied through regular technical updates.
- **Low Power, High Interest** stakeholders (Passengers, Bus Driver, Customer Support Staff) should be kept informed through surveys, feedback sessions, and training.
- **Low Power, Low Interest** stakeholders (External Vendors, System Testers) require minimal communication.

By implementing the recommended communication strategies, the project team can ensure that all stakeholders are appropriately engaged, their concerns are addressed, and the Bus Ticketing System is developed successfully to meet the needs of all parties involved.
